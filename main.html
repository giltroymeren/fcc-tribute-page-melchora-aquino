Melchora Aquino de Ramos

The Grand Woman of the Philippine Revolution

The façade of the Tandang Sora Memorial Shrine as seen in the Himlayang Pilipino National Park[4].

Here's a simple timeline of Melchora Aquino's life (and posthumous recognitions):

  • 1812 - Born in Balintawak, Quezon City[5].
  • 1896 - Philippine Revolution against Spaniards began. Offered her store to aid revolutionaries through medical aid, moral support and shelter. This led to her being known as "Tandang Sora" and "Mother of the Philippine Revolution" and other similar titles[5].
  • 1903 - Returned to Balintawak by Americans from being exiled by the Spaniard during the Revolution[2].
  • 1919 - Died in the Balintawak at the age of 107[2][5].
  • 1947 - Given tribute through the film "Tandang Sora" by director Lamberto Avelana and portrayed by actress Rosa del Rosario[1].
  • 1951 - Featured as the first Filipina on a Philippine peso banknote[5][1].
  • 2011 - Quezon City declared 2012 "Tandang Sora Year"[2][5].

Sa paglalagak natin ng mga labi ni Tandang Sora sa kaniyang bagong dambana, nawa'y magsilbing paalala sa bawat dumadalaw dito ang kakaibang uri ng kabayanihan ni Melchora Aquino: na kung gusto mo talagang pagsilbihan ang bayan, hindi hadlang ang edad, o ang kasarian, o anupamang balakid sa iyong harapan. Kaya naman tulungan natin ang ating kapwa, at nagkakaisa tayong kumilos para sa kapakanan ng ating bansa. Sa ganitong paraan, ipinapakita nating buhay pa rin ang pamana ni Melchora Aquino; at buhay pa rin ang kabayanihan at pagbabayanihan sa puso ng bawat Pilipino.
